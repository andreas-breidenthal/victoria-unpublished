The Anomaly

Chapter 22

I was in ten minutes before the lift. The room woke in stages: lights; a monitor glow; the dry click of a barcode gun checking its own pulse.

At eight-thirty the doors opened, and the pre-1914 trolley arrived — two tiers, packets stacked like careful bread. The top slip read MISC. (pre-1914) in the same lazy hand as yesterday.

The third packet came up with a label that didn't want to say anything. Misc. Corr. — Various (pre-war), the modern type said. Under it, erased hard, the older line showed if you leaned it to the light: Miscell. — Non-trade (letters). The outer folder was standard. Inside, halfway down, sat a secondary wrapper — not house stock, not trade, too stiff for these shelves — tied once with old cotton and tucked flat.

“Hold,” I said, and the operator froze the way good hands freeze: everything where it is, nothing strained.

I untied the cotton and eased the wrapper open. Inside: a thin sheaf without company stationery, no docketing, no trade headings. Typescript — later than the ledgers around it — scored over in pencil that didn't belong to an office. The top right of the first leaf carried a single “1” in graphite. The twelfth — because there were twelve — carried “12.”

I didn't read. I looked. Wove paper; no house forms; no inward/outward marks. Margins carried instructional pencil — decisive, not hesitant. The headings gave the window: June–July 1837.

“Non-trade by content and medium,” I said. “Reads like an amateur editorial pastiche — pseudo-documentary voice. Out of scope for this series. Low risk: no PII, no suppliers, no pricing, no BBR letterhead.”

The client archivist wrote in the incident book — neat, unfussy. We followed the protocol. No leaf photography. No content images. The archivist said it again for the log: “No page-level capture authorised.”

She slid a form toward me. “TCI. Fourteen days, no publicity, no page-level imaging, preserve and return on demand, no title conveyed.” I read, signed, dated.

At the door, the evening felt ordinary on purpose. In the bag, the weight wasn't heavy. It was exact.
